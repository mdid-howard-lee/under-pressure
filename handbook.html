How to survive the semester.

This page is for players who want to understand the actual game underneath the vibe: turn-based events, card decisions, resource pressure, and branching outcomes.

Overview

A card game about managing pressure, not defeating monsters.

Players choose a student and move through one semester. Each event represents a school-life challenge: exams, group projects, criticism, family expectations, relationship shifts, or burnout. The player uses cards to respond, but every response changes status values and future storylines.

Core Loop
Choose CharacterFace EventPlay CardsUpdate StatusUnlock Outcome

The game is played in turns. One event equals one round. The goal is not to make perfect decisions; the goal is to survive the consequences of imperfect decisions.

Status Values
Sanity

Your emotional stability. If it collapses, choices become limited.

Academic

Your progress in study-related events.

Relationship

Your social support and trust with other students.

Energy

Your ability to keep acting before exhaustion hits.

Hover a card type to preview its design purpose.

End Conditions

Win

Survive the semester with enough Academic progress, Sanity, and Relationships to reach a stable ending.

Lose

Sanity collapses, Academic progress falls too far behind, or key relationships break before the final event.
